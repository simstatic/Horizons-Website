Introducing the SnowBot

Convenient. Easy to Use. Automated.

Our Story

The SnowBot was created by a team of four proactive Materials Scientists at Imperial College.

Two of our members who are Polish reflected on the daily, inconvenient and strenuousness of removing snow from the tops of their cars, before they could drive anywhere in Winter in Poland. We discovered that across this was problem across Europe and many other parts of the world (although not a significant problem in the UK) due driving safety laws.

After discovering that no automated snow-removing solution currently exists, the team decided to set about designing and prototyping the world’s first robot which could be used by people of all ages and abilities to start their day in a hassle free way. Special prominence was placed on making the device easy use, store and operate, to make it truly accessible for those with reduce mobility and or limited motion.

Features of the SnowBot How It Makes Your Mornings Even Better

Product Description

The SnowBot is an automated robot designed to remove snow from the top of consumer vehicles such as cars. It eliminates the time and strain associated with removing snow from a vehicle; is accessible to those who would struggle to use existing snow clearing tools; and is an affordable a modern solution to an age-old problem.

SnowBot Features

  • Magnetic tracks to grip the robot to car rooftops
  • Silicon lined snow plow to push snow across the surface of the car without scratching the car paintwork
  • Battery powered with rechargeable batteries (batteries must be recharged separately)
  • Robust steel bodied frame housing electrical components & circuitry
  • Sensors to detect the edge of the car and create the optimum snow clearing path

What makes a good website?

In my opinion a good website is one which is functional, easy to use and is aesthetically pleasing.

First and foremost, the website should be functional; it should aid the user in carrying out its purpose. For online stores, this will include having an effective ecommerce network where customers can view products, add them to the cart and purchase the products. For other websites, the function may simply be to give the user information, in which case it should be very clear how to navigate the website to find the relevant information.

This ties in to point 2; the website interface should be easy to navigate with an intuitive layout. It should also be accessible for all ages, abilities in its intended audience. For instance, if it is a website for English speakers, all content and navigation tools should be in English, while if is supposed to be used by both French and English speakers, it should have a dedicated English/French toggle where users can switch to their language of choice. Other features that aid ease of use include suitable text size and fonts and presenting information in a coherent manner that is straightforward for the reader to take in and process (using a moderate number of animations, and avoiding clashing background and text colours).

Finally, a website should be aesthetically pleasing. This is mostly a marketing feature, as ugly websites or basic websites seem outdated or untended to; if two companies offer the same service, but one’s website is better formatted and easier to use, that website will probably obtain a higher visit:action conversion rate.

Accessibility on Websites

Accessibility on websites is the idea of designing a website so that anyone can use in spite of their any conditions or impairments they may have. The most common form of impairments are vision, hearing, motor skills, photosensitive seizures and cognitive disabilities (including dementia and dyslexia). Assistive technologies exist to help users access the internet including screen readers which play text out loud, speech recognition software and alternative keyboards. An accessible website is one which doesn’t exclude groups of people who may have an impairment.

I have made this website accessible by ensuring it can be used without a mouse: it is navigable using just the tab key. I have tried to make sure the content is accessible by only using static content; *dynamic content is normally not read by screenreaders.

I have used headers across this website, which will help screen readers interpret the page. All images include alternative text landmarks which describe the image they are affiliated with – this allows screen readers to read the image.

I have also used made my use of colour accessible; colour blind individuals or those with visual impairments can struggle to read text/view images when there is poor colour contrast. Hence, I chose to use a high contrast palette, in addition to a moderate font size and easy to read font.

GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) was a piece of legislation introduced by the European Union (EU) in May 2018. It aims to offers increased protection of individuals’ personal data, and provide additional rights to consumers. While GDPR only applies to regarding individuals in the EU and European Economic Area (EEA), the global nature of business means that many companies outside of the EU/EEA are need to comply with GDPR.

Personal data is termed as any information which could be used to identify a person and includes their name, age, gender, usernames, location, IP address and other types of unique data. Companies operating within the EU need to prove that they have consent for a person’s data and describe how they intend to use the data. They must also delete data if it is no longer relevant or needed, and consumers have a right of access to know what data a company holds and who they passed this information onto.

Additionally, under GDPR, companies need to report data breaches within 72 hours of the breach being discovered, and the minimum threshold for personal data storage is more stringent than prior to GDPR. Non-compliance with GDPR can result in significant fines of up to 4% of a company’s global turnover.

I have ensured this website complies with GDPR by asking for the permission of my teammates to use their names and photographs on the website. Similarly, for the contact us form on this website, the user needs to give consent for their data to be used to contact them to answer their query.
